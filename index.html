Unseen Moments

A Collection of Overlooked Beauty

This is a collection of images that, when sharing with friends or family, I have skipped because I thought they didn't have the 'wow' factor. However, I decided to put this album together to show off the images and moments that may not look too special at first glance, but hold deeper meaning upon further thought or inspection. I hope you enjoy these moments.

Photo 001: Matias & Evan enjoying the weather

I like this image because I think it does a good job of showing everyone (left, Matias; right, Evan) just enjoying the moment on vacation, which can be often overlooked in the busy times of travel.

Photo 002: Man in the alley

This image is cool to me because at the time, I was amazed by how the people lived in this town (Tepoztlán). The shops were on the first floor, and their houses were right above them, and every day, they would open their shop, and work until close. Seeing this man in the alley grabbing items for his shop really made me stop to think about how different our lives really are, and it was here where I began to truly apreciate the culture and community I was exploring.

Photo 003: Evidence of breakfast

This was a picture of Matias' egg shell basket in Tepoztlán. While kind of cool to look at you probably wouldn't look at this too long. However to me this image was, maybe not proof per say but showed that the people here live in this community every day and it is probably all they think of, just how all I think about is my next homework assingment. It made me wonder if I had a choice, which would I choose; it made me more aware of the kinds of things I can pay attention to both while on vacation there, but also for when I got back home.

Photo 004: Looking back as the church

I took this picture after appreciating a beautiful church in Tepoztlán. Often, you will just see pictures of the church, but I thought at the time that while the church is powerful, being able to be present and appreciative of the moment is equally important. This picture is like the eye of the church looking out at all the poeple.

Photo 005: Mountainside View

Okay, I will admit this one does look pretty good, and I did end up showing it to a couple people, but I included it in this project page because of the fact that Matias (who grew up here), along with those in the town didnt seem to bat an eye at the beautiful mountain ranges surrounding them. It made me think about how we often take our surroundings for granted, and how important it is to appreciate the beauty in our everyday lives.

Photo 007: Ian's Eye

This is just a close up picture of Ian's eye that I dont feel like a lot of people would really care to see on social media, but I do think that it is a cool shot. I also took this when I first got my camera so it was cool to see what it was capable of.

Photo 007: Morning Mist

I took this in the early morning on a hike up Mt. Washburn. When I first took it, I thought it was too blurry and it never left my folder, but through looking back at these pictures I feel like it really isnt about what you can see in picture, but rather about what the picture makes you feel. Maybe its just becuase of my fond memories of the trip, but this picture really does a good job of summing up that trip for me.

Photo 008: Old & New

Upon first glance, there isnt a whole lot to look at with this picture, and thats why it never really left my folder until now. However, I think it does a neat job of showing how two generations experience the same scene; my two sisters (left, Emme; right, Lily) are looking through their phones, I look through my camera, and my dad simply enjoys what is in front of him. I think there might be something to say there.

Photo 009: Reflection

This image is a little over-exposed which is why I never shared it anywhere, but I thought that the reflection from the water was really cool. If you want to get philosphical with it, it almost could represent how you make such an impact with your wake, but if you never stop to look at your place around you, you will only ever see yourself and never how you affect others.

Photo 010: Above you

This is an image from state street, and while it is a cool shot, I thought it a little bland. I decided to include it here because this is a view of the buildings above that I don't think I would have ever really paid attention to if I wasn't out looking for things to take pictures of that day.
